AMENDMENT 1
Hot Deals Rotating Ticker Bar — Premium & Affiliate-Priority Merchant Listings
1 New Adds to SRD Section: Section 2.1 — Homepage, immediately below the
Navbar
Amendment 1: Hot Deals Rotating Ticker Bar
The homepage must include a full-width, auto-scrolling Hot Deals ticker bar positioned immediately below
the navigation bar — the first thing a customer sees before the hero section. This bar serves two functions
simultaneously: it provides customers with a live, rotating feed of the hottest current deals, and it gives
premium-paying merchants priority visibility on the platform's most-trafficked screen real estate.
1.1 Visual Design & Position
PLACEMENT & VISUAL SPEC
Position: immediately below the navbar, full viewport width. Fixed/sticky — stays visible as the
user scrolls down the page. Height: 44px desktop, 40px mobile.
Background: deep navy (#1A3C5E) with a subtle shimmer/gloss effect. Left label badge: '🔴 HOT
DEALS' in orange pill with white text, left-aligned. Deals scroll from right to left in a continuous
loop (CSS marquee animation or JS-driven carousel).
Each deal item in the ticker: Brand logo (16px circle) + Brand name (bold white) + offer snippet
(e.g. 'Flat 30% off') + discount badge (orange pill) + '→' arrow. Items separated by a vertical
orange dividing line.
Ticker scroll speed: smooth continuous scroll, completing a full loop approximately every 20–25
seconds. Pauses on hover (desktop) and on touch-hold (mobile).
Clicking any item in the ticker opens the individual deal page (/deal/:id). The ticker must be fully
clickable — each item is a link.
'View All Hot Deals →' link at the far right of the ticker bar (fixed position, does not scroll with the
marquee). Links to /deals?filter=featured.
1.2 Content Source & Priority Logic
The deals shown in the ticker bar are NOT random. They follow a strict priority hierarchy that directly
supports the platform's revenue model:
PRIORITY HIERARCHY — TICKER CONTENT (highest to lowest priority)
PRIORITY 1 — Affiliate Marketing / Paid Featured Slots: Merchants who have purchased a
'Homepage Featured Slot' add-on (₹999/3 days) or a 'Flash Campaign Boost' (₹799/campaign) get
their deals shown first in the ticker. Admin can manually reorder these in the admin panel.

PRIORITY 2 — Enterprise Merchants: All active coupons/deals from Enterprise plan merchants
appear next, automatically and continuously.
PRIORITY 3 — Pro Merchants: Active coupons from Pro plan merchants with the highest
redemption rates (last 7 days) fill remaining ticker slots.
PRIORITY 4 — Growth Merchants: Top-performing coupons from Growth plan merchants fill
remaining slots, sorted by click-through rate.
PRIORITY 5 — Starter Merchants: Starter merchant coupons appear only if ticker slots are unfilled
(edge case — unlikely once platform has 20+ paid merchants).
Ticker content updates in real-time from the database — no manual curation required except for
PRIORITY 1 paid slots which are managed from the admin panel.
Admin panel must have a 'Ticker Management' section: view current ticker order, manually
pin/unpin deals, see which merchants have paid for featured placement, and override the
automatic priority order for specific deals.
1.3 Merchant Dashboard Integration
MERCHANT-SIDE VISIBILITY
In the merchant dashboard → Analytics page, add a 'Ticker Impressions' KPI card showing how
many times the merchant's deals have appeared in the homepage ticker this month.
In the Subscription & Billing → Add-Ons section, the 'Homepage Featured Slot' add-on card must
explicitly state: 'Your deals appear first in the Hot Deals ticker bar for 3 consecutive days.'
When a merchant purchases a Featured Slot, their deals are automatically elevated to Priority 1
in the ticker for the purchased period. No manual admin action required.

AMENDMENT 2
Dedicated Expired Coupon Revival Entry Point on Homepage
2 New Adds to SRD Section: Section 2.1 — Homepage, as a standalone prominent
section
Amendment 2: Expired Coupon Revival — Homepage Section
SRD v1.0 includes the /expired-coupon-revival page and a brief mention within the 'How It Works' section
callout box. This Amendment requires a dedicated, visually prominent full-width Revival section on the
homepage — not merely a callout box inside another section. The Revival feature is Vouchiqo's strongest
differentiator and must be given its own hero-level treatment on the homepage.
2.1 Homepage Revival Section Specification
PLACEMENT
Insert between the 'How It Works' section and the 'Trending Categories Grid' section. This
positions it after the user understands how the platform works, maximising conversion intent.
VISUAL DESIGN
Full-width section. Background: deep navy (#1A3C5E) with a subtle gradient to dark teal. NOT the
same colour as the hero — use a different navy shade to visually separate it.
Left side (55% width): content panel. Right side (45% width): animated visual.
Left content: Small pill badge at top: '🔴 INDIA'S ONLY EXPIRED COUPON REVIVAL' (orange
background, white text). Main heading (large, white, bold): 'Your Expired Coupon Has Value.
Claim It.' Subheading (white, smaller): 'Don't throw away your expired discount codes.
Vouchiqo's Revival system reconnects you with the merchant and recovers your savings — 48
hours, zero cost to you.' Three stat points in a row: '5,000+ Revivals Processed | ₹25L+
Recovered | 78% Approval Rate' (placeholder stats, updatable from admin panel).
Inline mini-form within this section (does NOT require navigating to a separate page — it opens
in a slide-down expansion below the section or a modal): Expired Coupon Code (text input),
Brand/Store Name (text with autocomplete), Your Email (text input), 'Revive My Coupon' orange
pill button. On submit: success state shown inline — 'Submitted! We'll reach out within 48
hours.' Full form mirrors the /expired-coupon-revival page form.
Right visual: animated SVG or CSS illustration of a coupon with a cracked/expired visual
transforming into a fresh glowing coupon with a green checkmark. Loop animation,
approximately 3 second cycle.
Below the section: text link 'Learn how Revival works →' pointing to /expired-coupon-revival for
users who want full details before submitting.
MOBILE LAYOUT
Stack to single column. Visual moves below the form. Mini-form inputs stack vertically. Button
full width.

AMENDMENT 3
Customer Interest & Reference Selection — Personalised Deals Feed
3 New Adds to SRD Section: Section 2.1 (Homepage) + Section 2.8 (Customer Auth)
+ Section 2.9 (Profile)
Amendment 3: Customer Interest & Preference Selection
The platform must personalise the deals experience based on what each customer is actually interested in
buying. SRD v1.0 includes interest selection during signup, but this Amendment specifies three additional
touchpoints where preferences are captured and applied: a homepage first-visit prompt, a dedicated
preference widget for logged-in users, and a visible personalisation indicator on the deals feed.
3.1 First-Visit Interest Capture (Homepage)
FIRST-TIME VISITOR PROMPT
For first-time visitors (no account, no cookie), show a non-blocking interest selection
overlay/slide-in banner at the bottom of the page (NOT a full-screen modal that blocks browsing).
It appears after 10 seconds on the homepage or after the user scrolls 40% down the page,
whichever comes first.
Banner content: 'What are you shopping for today? Select your interests for personalised deals.'
Followed by a horizontal scrollable row of category chips with emoji icons — same 16 categories
as the category strip. Multi-select (user can pick 1 or more). CTA: 'Show Me Deals →' orange
button.
On selection: interests saved to localStorage (for anonymous session) and the deals feed on the
homepage reorders to show selected categories first. Banner dismissed.
'No thanks, show everything' text link to dismiss without selecting. Dismissed state saved in
localStorage — banner does not reappear for 30 days.
For logged-in users: banner does not appear. Their preferences are loaded from their profile
instead.
3.2 Logged-In User Preference Widget (Homepage)
PERSONALISED HOMEPAGE FEED
When a user is logged in, the homepage deals section heading changes from 'Today's Top Deals'
to 'Your Personalised Deals, [First Name]'. A small 'Edit Preferences' link appears next to the
heading.
The deals grid below this heading is filtered and sorted based on the user's saved interest
categories from their profile. Deals from non-selected categories are shown further down or in a
separate 'More Deals' section below.
'Edit Preferences' link opens a slide-in side panel with the full interest checkbox grid. User saves
changes and feed refreshes instantly without page reload.

AMENDMENT 4
Location Selection — Integrated Into Homepage & Main Deals Feed
4 Updated Adds to SRD Section: Section 2.1 (Homepage) + Section 2.6 (Nearby Offers)

Amendment 4: Customer Location Selection
SRD v1.0 includes the Nearby Offers page with map and location detection. This Amendment adds location
selection directly into the homepage experience so users do not need to navigate to a separate page to see
location-relevant deals. Location must be a persistent, always-visible setting across the entire customer
experience.
4.1 Location Selector — Persistent Homepage Element
LOCATION BADGE IN NAVBAR (always visible)
Add a location indicator to the right side of the navbar (between the search bar and the Login
button). Display: 🔴 [City Name] with a small chevron-down. Default state for new users: '🔴 Set
Location'. For users with a saved location: shows their city (e.g. '🔴 Ranchi').
Clicking the location badge opens a dropdown/popover with: 'Use My Current Location' (GPS
button with location pin icon) | City search input (type to search from list of 200+ Indian cities) |
State dropdown as alternative. Selecting a location saves it to the user's session (localStorage
for anonymous, database for logged-in users) and refreshes the deals feed instantly.
The selected location persists across all pages and sessions until changed. On return visits, the
last selected location is remembered.
HOMEPAGE DEALS FEED — LOCATION INTEGRATION
When a location is set, the homepage deals section shows a location-aware tab switcher below
the section heading: [🔴 Near [City]] [🔴🔴 All India]. Default active tab is 'Near [City]' when location
is set, 'All India' when no location set.
'Near [City]' tab: shows deals from merchants who have listed their city/state as matching the
user's location, PLUS all online/nationwide deals. Local deals are visually distinguished with a '🔴
Local Deal' orange badge on the card.
'All India' tab: shows all deals nationwide without location filtering — same as the current deals
feed.
4.2 Location Prompt for New Visitors
FIRST-VISIT LOCATION PROMPT
For users with no saved location, add a gentle location prompt after the hero section: a singleline bar (not a modal) with a location pin icon and text: 'See deals near you — set your location'
with a [Set Location] orange text button inline. This is separate from and stacked above the
Interest Capture banner (Amendment 3).

Customer Personal Savings Dashboard — Spending vs Savings Analytics
6 New Adds to SRD Section: Section 2.9 of SRD v1.0 — New tab within Customer

Amendment 7: Customer Personal Savings Dashboard
SRD v1.0 Section 2.9 defines the customer profile with tabs for Saves, Cashback Wallet, Activity, Nearby,
and Settings. This Amendment adds a dedicated Savings Dashboard tab — a personal analytics screen
showing the customer a clear picture of how much they have saved through Vouchiqo, what they have
spent, and the financial value the platform has delivered to them. This is a key retention and engagement
feature — users who see their savings visualised stay on the platform.
6.1 Savings Dashboard Tab Specification
TAB PLACEMENT & TITLE
Add as the FIRST tab in the customer profile (/profile). Tab label: '🔴 My Savings'. Replaces the
current first tab 'My Saves' — which moves to second position and is renamed 'Saved Deals'.
TOP KPI CARDS — 4 cards in a row (2×2 on mobile)
Card 1 — Total Saved This Month: large number in green (e.g. '₹1,240'), label 'Saved This Month',
small text 'from X coupon uses'. Trend indicator: +X% vs last month.
Card 2 — Total Saved All Time: large number (e.g. '₹8,450'), label 'Total Lifetime Savings'. Sub:
'Since [account creation date]'.
Card 3 — Total Spent (via Vouchiqo-tracked purchases): large number (e.g. '₹23,800'), label 'Total
Spend Tracked', small text 'across X purchases'. Note: this tracks the purchase amounts from
CoupX-tracked affiliate transactions only — not all spending. Tooltip explains this.
Card 4 — Savings Rate: percentage shown large (e.g. '34%'), label 'Your Savings Rate', sub text
'You save ₹34 for every ₹100 spent through Vouchiqo'. Green if above 20%, amber if 10–20%,
grey if below 10%.
SAVINGS TIMELINE CHART
Line chart showing month-by-month savings over the last 12 months. X-axis: months. Y-axis: ₹
saved. Orange line for savings, navy line for spending (both on same chart, dual Y-axis). Toggle
buttons: 3 Months | 6 Months | 12 Months | All Time.
Hover tooltip on any data point: 'In [Month]: Spent ₹X, Saved ₹Y across Z coupons.'
CATEGORY BREAKDOWN
Donut chart: 'Where I Save the Most' — savings broken down by category (Fashion, Electronics,
Food, etc.). Each category: a slice with label and ₹ amount. Clicking a slice filters the transaction
table below to that category.
Bar chart below donut: 'Top 5 Brands I've Saved With' — horizontal bars showing ₹ saved per
brand. Brand logo on each bar.
