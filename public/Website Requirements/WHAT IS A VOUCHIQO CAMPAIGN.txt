WHAT IS A VOUCHIQO CAMPAIGN? 

A Campaign on Vouchiqo is a structured, time-bound promotional event created by a merchant to achieve 
a specific business objective — more than just having a coupon code discoverable on the platform. When 
a merchant runs a Campaign, Vouchiqo activates a coordinated set of visibility actions across the 
platform: the deal is featured in the homepage ticker, pushed to relevant subscribers via email, sent as a 
push notification to opted-in users, elevated in the category page, and given a visible urgency indicator 
(countdown timer or "X uses remaining" counter) on the deal card itself. 
The 7 Campaign Types on Vouchiqo 
Each type is designed for a specific business situation. Merchants choose the type when creating a 
campaign, and the type determines the default duration, the urgency format, and which platform 
placements are activated. 


TYPE 1 — FLASH SALE CAMPAIGN 
What it is: The most time-compressed campaign type. A deep discount offered for a very short window 
— minimum 2 hours, maximum 48 hours. Designed to create maximum urgency and drive an immediate 
spike in traffic and redemptions. 
When to use it: Slow weekday, sudden excess inventory, a competitor just ran a sale and the merchant 
needs to respond, or a merchant wants to test whether a particular offer format drives volume before 
committing to a longer promotion. 
Merchant sets: Discount depth (typically deeper than their standard listing — e.g. 30% vs the usual 
15%), duration, total usage cap, and whether it's new-user-only or open to all. 
Platform behaviour: The deal is elevated to the top of the Flash Deals homepage section with a live 
countdown timer. It appears in the ticker with a "    
Flash" badge. If the merchant has a Push 
Notification add-on or included in their plan, a push goes out at campaign start. Email blast is optional. 
Duration: 2 hours to 48 hours. Anything longer becomes a Seasonal campaign. 



TYPE 2 — FESTIVAL CAMPAIGN 
What it is: A longer-format campaign tied to a specific Indian festival or cultural event. Pre-planned, 
thematically branded, and aligned with the peak spending period around the festival. This is the highest
revenue campaign type for most merchants. 
The festivals Vouchiqo supports with dedicated campaign templates: Diwali, Chhath Puja (especially 
relevant for Jharkhand/Bihar merchants), Holi, Navratri, Durga Puja, Eid, Christmas and New Year, 
Independence Day, Republic Day, Valentine's Day, Raksha Bandhan, Bhai Dooj, and Back-to-School 
(July–August). Each has a pre-built campaign theme, suggested discount range, and recommended 
duration based on that festival's typical spending pattern. 
What makes it different from a Flash Sale: Duration is longer (3–7 days), the campaign card has 
festival-specific visual treatment (Diwali themed card, Chhath themed card), and there is a pre-festival 
teaser period where Vouchiqo promotes "Coming Soon" teasers 3 days before the campaign launches — 
building anticipation before the deal even goes live. 
When to use it: Any merchant with seasonal sales patterns — home improvement merchants before 
Diwali renovation season, restaurants before Chhath, fashion boutiques before festivals. For Marbella 
specifically, Diwali and the pre-monsoon renovation season are the two biggest windows. 


TYPE 3 — NEW USER ACQUISITION CAMPAIGN 
What it is: A campaign specifically designed to bring in first-time customers — people who have never 
bought from that merchant before. The coupon is technically a "New Users Only" type, meaning it 
validates at checkout only on the first order. 
What Vouchiqo does differently for this type: The campaign is targeted exclusively to Vouchiqo users 
who have NOT previously clicked through to that merchant's brand page. These are genuinely new 
potential customers for the merchant. The campaign card is labelled "First-Time Offer" in teal — a 
distinct visual treatment that signals exclusivity. 
When to use it: A new business that just onboarded on Vouchiqo and needs to build a customer base fast. 
A merchant expanding to a new city or category. A business that has relied on word-of-mouth and wants 
to systematically acquire new customers. 
Merchant value: This is the closest thing to a guaranteed new customer acquisition campaign. The 
merchant pays only for the discount given (via CPA) — not for impressions or clicks. Every redemption 
is a new customer. 



TYPE 4 — SEASONAL / CLEARANCE CAMPAIGN 
What it is: A medium-duration campaign tied to a season change or stock clearance event — not a 
specific festival, but a recurring business cycle. Examples: End-of-Summer Flooring Sale, Monsoon 
Ready Plumbing Specials, Winter Collection Launch, Back-to-School Stationery Deals. 
Duration: 7 to 21 days. Longer than a flash sale, shorter than an always-on listing. Long enough to reach 
users who are in the purchase consideration phase, short enough to maintain urgency. 
Platform behaviour: Featured in the relevant category page for the full duration. Included in the weekly 
email digest for that category for the campaign weeks. No ticker priority unless the Flash Campaign 
Boost add-on is purchased. 
When to use it: Merchants with inventory they need to move before a season ends. Merchants with 
strong seasonal demand patterns. For Marbella — a "Pre-Monsoon Waterproofing Specials" campaign in 
May/June would be a perfect Seasonal Campaign. 



TYPE 5 — LOYALTY / RETURNING CUSTOMER CAMPAIGN 
What it is: A campaign targeted exclusively at users who have previously used a Vouchiqo coupon from 
that merchant. The platform identifies these users through redemption history and serves them the 
campaign deal with a "Welcome Back" framing. 
What it solves: Customer retention. Acquiring a new customer costs 5–7x more than retaining an 
existing one. A Returning Customer campaign re-activates users who tried the merchant once but haven't 
been back. 
How targeting works: Vouchiqo's system filters the campaign audience to only users who have at least 
one confirmed redemption from that merchant in the last 90 days. These users receive a personalised 
push: "Hi, it's been a while since you last used [Brand]. Here's something for you: [Exclusive returning 
customer offer]." 
When to use it: Every 60–90 days, once a merchant has accumulated 50+ redemption records. Creates a 
retention loop without the merchant needing to run their own CRM. 




TYPE 6 — BUNDLE / BOGO CAMPAIGN 
What it is: A campaign built around a non-standard discount format — Buy One Get One, bundle 
packages, free gift with purchase, or value-add offers (e.g. "Buy tiles worth ₹10,000 and get free grouting 
service worth ₹2,000"). These are not percentage-off or flat-off deals — they are structured promotional 
packages. 
Why it needs its own campaign type: The value communication is more complex. The deal card for a 
BOGO campaign displays differently — it shows "BUY 1 GET 1 FREE" in large text rather than a 
percentage badge. The redemption mechanism is also different — some BOGO deals require the 
customer to show the page in-store rather than entering a code at online checkout. 
When to use it: Food businesses (restaurant BOGO on specific dish), beauty salons (free hair wash with 
any colour service), home improvement retailers (free installation with product purchase), clothing 
boutiques (BOGO on selected season-end styles). 




TYPE 7 — REVIVAL CAMPAIGN (PRO AND ENTERPRISE ONLY) 
What it is: A campaign that is not about a new discount — it is about re-activating multiple expired 
codes at once and marketing that revival as a promotional event. "Missed it? It's back — for 48 hours 
only." The merchant selects a batch of their most-used expired codes, sets new expiry dates, and launches 
a Revival Campaign. 
Why this is powerful: Regular Revival (from the merchant dashboard) revives one code at a time in 
response to a customer request. A Revival Campaign is proactive — the merchant decides to revive their 
best-performing old codes and use the revival itself as a marketing hook. "Second Chance Sale" is a 
compelling narrative that other platforms cannot replicate. 
Platform treatment: Revival Campaign deals get a special "     
Second Chance" badge on the deal card. 
They appear in the Expired Coupon Revival section of the homepage and in the /expired-coupon-revival 
page as "Active Revival Campaigns" — a separate curated list that customers on the revival page see first. 
When to use it: Post-festival (the month after Diwali, when customers missed the festival deal and still 
want the discount). Post-season (customers who were waiting before buying now see the discount return). 
It creates a predictable annual promotional cycle: original campaign → expired → revival campaign 30 
days later.
