Document Purpose & Scope 
This document is an addendum to SRD v1.0 and Amendment v1.1. It supersedes and significantly expands 
Section 2.7 (Expired Offer Revival Page) and Section 3.6 (Expired Offer Revival Management) of SRD v1.0. 
The original spec assumed every expired-offer submission relates to a current Vouchiqo merchant. This 
addendum extends the system to accept submissions for ANY expired offer — including offers from merchants 
who have never been on Vouchiqo, or who were on Vouchiqo and have since left. 
�
� CRITICAL  This is Vouchiqo's second-largest USP after verification itself. The system must be built 
exactly as specified — it serves THREE simultaneous business functions: (1) genuine customer service 
(helping a customer recover a discount), (2) merchant win-back automation for churned Vouchiqo 
merchants, and (3) a structured lead-generation database for merchants who have never been on the 
platform. All three functions must work from a SINGLE customer-facing submission form — the complexity 
is handled entirely in backend routing logic, not by asking the customer different questions for different 
scenarios. 
Three-Way Routing — The Core Logic 
Every submission is automatically classified into one of three categories the moment the Brand/Business 
Name field is matched against the merchant database. This classification determines everything downstream 
and must happen server-side, instantly, without the customer ever seeing or needing to know which category 
they fall into.

A.3  Submission Confirmation & Rate Limiting 
MUST 
On successful submission: instant on-screen confirmation + automated confirmation 
email/WhatsApp (template per FAQ Library CANNED RESPONSE 'REVIVAL-01', already 
specified). Confirmation must state the same non-guarantee framing as the consent 
checkbox, briefly restated, plus an expected response window of 48 hours. 
 
MUST 
Rate limit: maximum 5 submissions per customer account per rolling 30-day period. On the 
6th attempt within the window, show a friendly block message rather than a hard error: 'You've 
reached your monthly submission limit. Need help sooner? Contact support.' with a support 
link. 
 
MUST 
Duplicate detection: if the same customer email + same Brand/Business Name combination is 
submitted again within 7 days of a prior submission, do NOT block it, but flag the new 
submission with an internal-only 'Possible Duplicate' tag visible in the admin queue (Part C) 
for a human glance before processing. 
 
A.4  Pre-Fill Behaviour for Known Listings 
MUST 
When a customer clicks 'Request Revival' from an already-expired listing on Vouchiqo (a 
listing that exists in Vouchiqo's own database, whether the merchant is currently active or 
churned), auto pre-fill Brand Name, Category, Discount Type, Discount Value, Description, 
and Expired Code directly from that stored listing data. Customer only needs to confirm/edit 
and fill in Group 3 and Group 4. 
 
SHOULD For pre-filled submissions from a still-active Category A merchant, allow an expedited single
click 'Yes, this is correct — submit' flow rather than re-displaying every field, to reduce friction 
for the highest-confidence, fastest-resolving case.

Part B: Merchant Module 
B.1  Revival Request Queue (Category A — Active Merchants) 
This is the existing Section 3.6 build from SRD v1.0, retained as specified, with the additions below. 
 
MUST 
Each incoming request displays: original offer details (pulled from Vouchiqo's stored listing if it 
originated there, or from the customer's submitted description if Category C/external), the 
customer's note on what they were trying to buy, exact days since expiry (calculated from the 
customer-submitted date field, not a vague label), and three action buttons. 
 
MUST Action 1 — Approve & Regenerate: merchant issues a fresh code and sets new validity period 
inline, without leaving the request screen. 
 
MUST Action 2 — Offer Alternative: merchant selects one of their OTHER currently active listings to 
link instead of issuing a new code. Must show a searchable list of the merchant's own active 
listings to pick from. 
 
MUST 
Action 3 — Decline: requires a mandatory short reason field (minimum 10 characters) before 
the decline can be submitted. This reason is what gets relayed to the customer per the 
consent checkbox they agreed to in Part A — declines without a reason are not permitted by 
the system. 
 
B.2  Auto-Approval Toggle (New — Addendum to SRD v1.0) 
SHOULD 
Add a setting in merchant Account Settings → Business Profile: 'Auto-approve revival 
requests within 30 days of expiry, same terms.' When enabled, any incoming Category A 
request where the customer's submitted expiry date is within the last 30 days, and the 
merchant has NOT manually customised regeneration terms before, is auto-approved using 
the original offer's exact terms and a system-generated new validity period (default: 14 days 
from approval). 
 
ℹ NOTE  This toggle is specifically for Pro and Enterprise merchants comfortable pre-authorising low-risk 
regenerations — it should be available but OFF by default for all plans, never auto-enabled.

MUST 
A single master table view showing ALL incoming submissions across all 3 categories, with 
columns: Submission Date, Customer Name, Brand Name, Category (A/B/C — colour-coded), 
Days Since Original Expiry, Priority (auto-calculated per C.2 rules below), Status, Assigned

MUST Default sort: Priority descending, then Days Since Expiry ascending (most urgent and most 
recoverable cases surface first). 
 
MUST Filter controls: by Category (A/B/C), by Merchant City, by Business Category (the 15 Vouchiqo 
categories), by Status, by 'Possible Duplicate' flag. 
 
C.2  Auto-Calculated Priority Logic — Build This Exactly 
Priority is system-calculated on submission, not manually set. The logic below must run automatically and 
assign one of three priority labels. 
 
Priority Auto-Assignment Rule 
HIGH Category A submissions (any). OR: any submission where (today's date − customer-submitted 
expiry date) ≤ 14 days. OR: customer has a prior submission with a resolved 'Code Regenerated' or 
'Alternative Offer Provided' outcome on file (repeat satisfied user). 
MEDIUM Category B submissions (any). OR: Category C submissions where Merchant City = a Phase 1 
priority city (configurable list, starts with Ranchi) AND Business Type is in the current priority 
category list (configurable, e.g. Home Improvement, Fashion, Beauty at launch). 
LOW All remaining Category C submissions — i.e. outside priority cities or outside priority categories. 
These still get logged into the Merchant Intelligence Database (C.4) automatically even at LOW 
priority; only the immediate customer-service action is deprioritised, not the data capture. 
 
MUST Priority list values (HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW priority city/category configuration referenced above) 
must be editable from the admin panel itself — not hardcoded — since priority 
cities/categories will change as Vouchiqo expands past Ranchi Phase 1.

C.3  Category-Specific Admin Workflows 
Category A Workflow (mostly automated) 
MUST 
No admin action needed in the normal case — request routes directly to merchant dashboard 
(Part B.1). Admin queue only surfaces a Category A item if the merchant has NOT responded 
within the SLA window (48 hours), at which point it appears in admin queue with a 'Merchant 
Non-Responsive — Needs Founder Follow-Up' flag, prompting the founder to personally 
chase the merchant. 
Category B Workflow (Win-Back) 
MUST 
Admin queue item shows: original merchant contact details (from their dormant account), their 
last subscription plan before churning, their last active listing data, and the new submission's 
details. 
MUST 
One-click action: 'Generate Win-Back Outreach' — auto-populates a pre-written message 
(editable before sending) using the merchant name, business type, and submission count, 
following the win-back script pattern from the Merchant Playbook. Sends via WhatsApp 
Business or email, logged against the merchant record. 
MUST 
Admin can manually mark outcome: Reactivated / Declined to Return / No Response — each 
updates the merchant record's status accordingly, and 'Declined to Return' triggers the Do
Not-Contact suppression in C.5. 
Category C Workflow (New Merchant Intelligence) 
MUST 
Admin queue item shows: the submitted business details, the source platform field (where 
customer found the offer), and crucially — the LIVE updated submission count for this exact 
business name if multiple customers have separately submitted for the same unlisted 
business (see C.4 for how this count is built). 
MUST 
One-click action: 'Add to Merchant Outreach List' — pushes this business into the Merchant 
Demand Report (C.4) as an active acquisition target if not already there, or increments its 
existing record.

C.5  Do-Not-Contact Suppression 
MUST 
When a Category B or C business record has its Do-Not-Contact Flag set to true, that 
business must NOT be auto-surfaced in any future 'Generate Win-Back Outreach' or 'Add to 
Merchant Outreach List' one-click actions, even if new customer submissions continue to 
increment its Submission Count. The record still updates silently in the background (count, 
source platforms, etc.) for founder reference, but no outreach action is auto-suggested. 
 
MUST Admin must be able to manually override and re-enable contact on a flagged record (e.g. if 
significant time has passed or circumstances changed) — this is a soft suppression, not a 
permanent block, but it requires a deliberate admin action to reverse. 
 
C.6  Alternative-Offer Suggestion Engine 
Supports Outcome 2 (Part A.5) — when a regeneration isn't possible, the admin (or auto-approval logic) needs 
to quickly check whether a reasonable substitute offer exists. 
 
MUST 
When processing a decline for any category, the admin panel must auto-display up to 3 
suggested alternative active listings, ranked by: (1) same merchant if they have any other 
active listing, then (2) same Business Category + same City, then (3) same Business 
Category nationally. Suggestions pull from Discount Type and approximate Discount Value 
proximity to the original submitted offer. 
 
SHOULD 
If zero reasonable alternatives exist (common in early Phase 1 with a small merchant base), 
the system should clearly show 'No alternative available' rather than forcing a poor-fit 
suggestion — admin should never be pressured by the UI into offering an irrelevant substitute 
just to fill the Outcome 2 slot.

C.7  Public Revival Social-Proof Feed — Sourcing Rule 
SRD v1.0 Section 2.7 already specifies a 'Recent Revivals' social-proof feed on the public Revival page. This 
addendum adds a mandatory sourcing rule for what is allowed to populate it. 
�
� CRITICAL  The public feed MUST only ever be populated from submissions with a resolved outcome of 
'Code Regenerated' or 'Alternative Offer Provided' (genuine wins). It must NEVER show 'Respectfully 
Declined' cases. It must NEVER feature a Category C business by name unless that business has since 
actually converted to a listed Vouchiqo merchant (Outreach Status = Converted) — featuring a non
partner's brand name without consent is a real legal exposure, not just a courtesy issue. 
MUST 
Admin panel needs a manual toggle per resolved submission: 'Include in public Recent 
Revivals feed' (default OFF, must be explicitly turned on by admin after confirming the above 
sourcing rule is satisfied) — this should never be fully automatic given the legal sensitivity.

D.3  Data Retention for Churned Merchants 
MUST 
When a merchant's status changes to Inactive/Suspended/Trial Expired, their listing data 
(past offers, terms, categories) must be RETAINED in the database, not deleted — this is 
required for both the Win-Back pre-fill flow (Part B.3) and for matching future Category B 
submissions correctly. Standard customer account deletion rules (SRD Section 2.9 — 30-day 
recovery window, full deletion after) are separate and unaffected by this; this retention rule 
applies specifically to merchant listing/offer historical data, not personal customer data.

Why This Matters More Than It Looks 
Every other coupon platform treats an expired-code complaint as a dead end — a support 
ticket to close. This system turns that exact moment into three different assets simultaneously: 
a saved customer, a reactivated merchant, or a new sales lead with built-in evidence of 
demand. The Submission Count field in Part C.4 is the single most valuable number in this 
entire build — it is what lets a cold outreach call become a warm one.
